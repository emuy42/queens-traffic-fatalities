A decade after the launch of Vision Zero, traffic deaths are once again on the rise in Queens

The sharp rise of pedestrian and cyclist fatalities in Queens over the last two years matches a broader trend citywide. In New York, traffic deaths have increased after a low in 2018.

By Elisa Muyl

Dec. 14, 2023

Nine more pedestrians and cyclists have been killed in traffic accidents in Queens so far in 2023, compared to 2021.

The rise comes nearly a decade after then-mayor Bill DeBlasio rolled out Vision Zero in January 2014. The plan introduced broad changes to the way the city addressed street safety.

However, after initial progress, fatalities among bikers and walkers have risen citywide since 2018. New Yorkers are now almost as likely to die in a traffic crash as by gun violence, according to reporting by Gothamist.

Jon Orcutt, the director for advocacy at Bike New York, said that the biggest gap in New York’s Vision Zero plan is mayoral leadership.

“There hasn’t been any real political steam behind Vision Zero since about 2015 or ‘16,” said Orcutt, adding that DeBlasio’s interest died out after a few years.

“And Mayor Adams has zero interest in this stuff, actually,” he added.
